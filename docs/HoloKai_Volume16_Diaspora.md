### 16.1 Moors in Europe - Africa Civilizes Europe

#### Al-Andalus 711-1492
- moors: Berber + Arab Muslims from North Africa conquered Spain 711, ruled 800 years. Brought knowledge from Timbuktu, Cairo, Baghdad.
- cities: Cordoba population 500k 10th century, largest city in Europe, street lighting, running water, 70 libraries, university. London population 15k at same time. Library of Cordoba 400k books.
- knowledge_transfer: Translated Greek works preserved in Africa, plus African knowledge: medicine, mathematics (zero, algebra), astronomy (astrolabe), agriculture (orange, lemon, rice, cotton, sugar), architecture (horseshoe arch).
- scholars: Ibn Battuta (Morocco 1304-1369) traveled 120k km, Ibn Khaldun (Tunisia 1332-1406) father of sociology, Mansa Musa's hajj brought Mali gold to Europe maps.
- fall: Reconquista 1492, but many Moors stayed as Moriscos.

#### Black Romans & Greeks
- black_romans: Roman army had African legions, Septimius Severus African emperor from Libya 193-211 CE, born Leptis Magna Libya, ruled Rome, built Arch. His wife Julia Domna Syrian, son Caracalla.
- memnon: Greek mythology Memnon king of Ethiopia fought at Troy, described as beautiful.
- aethiopia: Greek word Aethiopia = land of burnt faces, used for Africa south of Sahara, in Homer's Odyssey gods feast with Ethiopians.

### 16.2 Zanj & Indian Ocean Diaspora

#### Zanj Empire
- zanj: Bantu from East Africa taken to Iraq, 15,000+ slaves revolted 869-883 CE, created state near Basra, defeated Abbasid caliphate for 14 years, called Zanj Rebellion, led by Ali ibn Muhammad. Largest slave revolt in Islamic history.
- siddi: Africans in India, 20,000+ in Gujarat/Karnataka, descendants of Bantu, became generals, rulers, e.g., Malik Ambar (1548-1626) Ethiopian slave became king of Ahmednagar India, built city, fought Mughals.
- omani_arabs: Swahili coast to Oman, many Africans in Oman.

### 16.3 Atlantic Diaspora - The Black Atlantic

#### Transatlantic Slave Trade
- numbers: 12.5M Africans shipped 1525-1866, 10.7M arrived, 2M died middle passage. Largest forced migration.
- cultures: Yoruba, Igbo, Akan, Fon, Kongo, Mandinka - brought Ifa, music, agriculture (rice, okra, yams), knowledge.
- brazil: 5M Africans to Brazil, largest diaspora, Candomble, Capoeira martial art disguised as dance, Samba.
- haiti: 1804 Haitian Revolution - first black republic, led by Toussaint Louverture, Jean-Jacques Dessalines, defeated Napoleon, inspired worldwide. Vodou.
- usa: Blues, jazz, rock, hip-hop all from African polyrhythm, banjo from ngoni, spirituals.
- maroons: Free communities: Jamaica Maroons (Nanny), Brazil Quilombo dos Palmares (Zumbi, 20k free Africans, resisted Portuguese 100 years), Suriname Saramaka.

### 16.4 Knowledge Impact

#### Contributions to World
- mathematics: Egyptian math -> Greek (Thales studied in Egypt), binary Ifa -> computer science.
- medicine: Egyptian medicine -> Greek Hippocrates, quinine from Africa?
- agriculture: Coffee Ethiopia, sorghum, millet fed world, rice knowledge brought to South Carolina by Africans (Gullah).
- architecture: Egyptian obelisks in Rome, Washington Monument modeled on Egyptian, Great Zimbabwe influence.
- university: Al-Qarawiyyin 859 founded by woman -> model for European universities.

### 16.5 Return & Pan-Africanism

#### Pan-Africanism
- garvey: Marcus Garvey 1914 Black Star Line, back to Africa.
- dubois: W.E.B. Du Bois 1900 Pan-African Congress.
- nkrumah: Kwame Nkrumah Ghana independence 1957, first sub-Saharan independent, Pan-Africanism.
- rastafari: Jamaica 1930s, Haile Selassie as God, Ethiopia as Zion, repatriation.

#### Modern Diaspora
- 170M people of African descent in Americas, 20M in Europe, 3M in India Siddis.
- Remittances: $50B/year to Africa.
