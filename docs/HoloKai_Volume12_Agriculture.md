### 12.1 Indigenous Crops - Africa Fed Itself
- sorghum: Domesticated 5000 BCE in Sudan/Chad, most important cereal in Africa, drought resistant, 50M tons/year today, originated in Africa.
- pearl_millet: Domesticated 4000 BCE in Mali, Niger, survives 250mm rain, staple Sahel.
- finger_millet: Domesticated 3000 BCE Ethiopia highlands, nutritious, high calcium.
- teff: Domesticated 4000 BCE Ethiopia, smallest grain in world, used for injera, high protein, gluten-free, now superfood.
- african_rice: Oryza glaberrima domesticated 1500 BCE in Niger River delta Mali, independent from Asian rice, 20,000 varieties, tolerant to drought, flood, pests.
- yam: White yam, yellow yam domesticated 8000 BCE in Niger River basin Nigeria/Cameroon, most important tuber West Africa, requires staking, yam festivals (New Yam Festival Igbo).
- cowpea: Black-eyed peas domesticated 4000 BCE West Africa, nitrogen-fixing, protein.
- oil_palm: Domesticated 5000 BCE Nigeria/Cameroon, source of palm oil (now world's most used oil), palm wine, palm kernels.
- okra: Domesticated Ethiopia/Egypt, brought to Americas via slave trade.
- watermelon: Domesticated 4000 BCE in Sudan/Egypt, wild Kordofan melon, depicted in Egyptian tombs.
- coffee: Coffea arabica discovered 850 CE Ethiopia, shepherd Kaldi saw goats energized, Sufi monks used, spread to Yemen 15th century, now second most traded commodity after oil. Coffee ceremony Ethiopia 1 hour ritual.

### 12.2 Animal Domestication
- cattle: African cattle (Bos taurus africanus) domesticated 8000 BCE in Sahara, independent from Near East, based on rock art, bones. Before desert. Long-horned.
- donkey: Domesticated 5000 BCE in Egypt/Nubia, from African wild ass, crucial for trade, transport.
- guinea_fowl: Domesticated 5000 BCE West Africa, now worldwide poultry.
- chicken: Brought from Asia but African breeds developed.

### 12.3 Agricultural Systems
- shifting_cultivation: Slash and burn, but with fallow, sustainable, used forest areas.
- terrace: Konso Ethiopia terraces 500 years old, UNESCO, stone walls prevent erosion, intensive farming.
- irrigation: Shaduf (Egyptian lever), foggara (Garamantes underground tunnels 600km), nilometer to measure flood.
- zai: Burkina Faso technique, pits to capture water, revived by Yacouba Sawadogo who stopped desert.
- intercropping: Mix crops (millet + cowpea + sorghum) = risk reduction, nitrogen fixing, studied as climate-smart agriculture now.

### 12.4 Food Culture & Preservation
- fermentation: Injera (teff fermented), ogi (fermented maize), iru (locust beans), palm wine.
- storage: Granaries - Dogon granaries cosmology, raised to prevent pests, mud + straw.
- spices: Grains of paradise (West Africa pepper), prekese, alligator pepper.
