### 14.1 Shango - The Thunder King

#### Who is Shango?
- orisha: Yoruba orisha of thunder, lightning, fire, drums, dance, justice. Fourth Alaafin (king) of Oyo Empire c.15th century, historical king who became deified.
- story: Shango was powerful but temperamental king, could summon lightning, had 3 wives: Oya (wind), Oshun (love), Oba (wife). Hanged himself after accidentally killing his people with lightning, then became orisha.
- symbols: Double-headed axe (oshe), Bata drums (double-headed), colors red and white, number 6.
- thunder_axe: Oshe Shango - double axe represents thunderstone (neolithic stone axe believed to be lightning bolt). Found after lightning strikes - actually Stone Age axes.

#### Shango in Diaspora
- cuba: Chango - Santeria, syncretized with Santa Barbara, red, thunder.
- brazil: Xango - Candomble, justice, fire.
- haiti: Shango - Vodou, thunder.
- usa: Still worshipped in Yoruba revival.

#### Thunder as Energy Concept
- african_physics: Thunder = energy, sound + light + power. Shango controls it = control of energy. Matches FEEXARA Thunder Code: energy, power, transformation.
- drumming: Bata drums mimic thunder - low bass = rumble, high = crack. Drummers are thunder-makers.
- dance: Shango dance is powerful, stomping, axe movements, embodies lightning.

### 14.2 Oya - Wind, Storms, Transformation

#### Who is Oya?
- orisha: Goddess of winds, storms, hurricanes, Niger River, transformation, guardian of cemetery. Wife of Shango.
- symbols: Fly whisk (irukere), buffalo horns (she can turn into buffalo), number 9, colors purple/wine, wind.
- power: Controls wind that brings Shango's lightning, represents change, rapid transformation, destruction that clears for new growth. Tornado, hurricane.
- diaspora: Oya in Santeria = Oya, syncretized with Virgin of Candelaria, owner of cemetery.

#### Oya & FEEXARA
- transformation: Oya = sudden change, innovation, disruption - startup energy.
- wind: Wind = invisible power, like code, like AI.

### 14.3 Ogun - Iron, Technology, War, Innovation

#### Who is Ogun?
- orisha: God of iron, war, hunting, technology, innovation, tools, blacksmiths. Most important for civilization - without iron no tools.
- story: Ogun cleared forest with iron tools for other orishas to create world. Lives in forest, powerful, honest, but can be destructive.
- symbols: Iron tools - machete, hoe, anvil, hammer, colors green/black, number 7, dog as animal.
- worship: Blacksmiths, drivers, mechanics, surgeons, anyone using iron prays to Ogun. Offerings: palm wine, dog (formerly).
- diaspora: Cuba Ogun, Brazil Ogum - still patron of workers.

#### Metallurgy Connection
- nok_iron: 500 BCE iron smelting, Haya steel preheating, Meroe industrial iron - all Ogun domain.
- blacksmith: Blacksmiths in many African societies are feared, powerful, can be kingmakers, control secret knowledge of fire, transformation of ore to iron = magic.
- innovation: Ogun = first technologist, first engineer. For HoloKai, Ogun = code, hardware, building.
