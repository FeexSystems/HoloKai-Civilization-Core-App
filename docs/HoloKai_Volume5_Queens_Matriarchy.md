### 5.1 Kandakes of Kush - Complete List
- Alakhebasken c.295 BCE - first Kandake known
- Shanakdakhete c.170-150 BCE - earliest queen regnant, depicted alone smiting enemies without king, holds spear, wears crown of Amun. First woman in Africa depicted ruling without husband.
- Amanirenas c.40-10 BCE - war vs Rome, blind one eye, attacked Elephantine, defaced Augustus statues, treaty with Rome as equal. Romans called her "Candace the one-eyed".
- Amanishakheto c.10 BCE-1 CE - pyramid M6, gold hoard 1000+ objects, armlets, necklaces weighing kg. Built temples at Wad ban Naqa.
- Amanitore c.1 BCE-20 CE - co-ruler with son Natakamani, built 100+ monuments, palace at Jebel Barkal.
- Amanikhatashan c.62-85 CE - sent cavalry to help Rome fight Jerusalem revolt per Nero.

### 5.2 Queen Mothers - The Power Behind Throne
- Akan Ohemaa: Owns stool, nominates king, can destool, controls own courts, has linguist, has female militia (Asafo). Example: Yaa Asantewaa was Ohemaa of Ejisu.
- Swazi Ndlovukati: Mother of Nation, co-ruler, controls rainmaking, national ritual Incwala, can veto king.
- Buganda Namasole: Mother of Kabaka, owns estates, has own palace, court, taxes.
- Yoruba Iya Oba: Mother of King, spiritual protector, controls Gelede masquerade to control male power.
- Mechanism: Matrilineal succession - kingship passes through sister's son, so queen mother's womb is source of legitimacy. This is why many African societies trace royalty through mother.

### 5.3 Warrior Queens - Detailed
- Ahhotep I c.1560-1530 BCE Egypt - warrior queen who rallied Egypt against Hyksos, awarded Order of the Fly (military decoration), her tomb had daggers, axes, golden flies.
- Hatshepsut c.1479-1458 BCE - took throne name Maatkare, built fleet, sent trade expedition, re-established army after Hyksos.
- Queen Idia of Benin c.1504-1550 - mother of Oba Esigie, first Queen Mother (Iyoba), went to war to secure son's throne, used esoteric knowledge, ivory mask is symbol of FESTAC. Portuguese traded with her.
- Nzinga of Ndongo/Matamba Angola 1583-1663 - spoke 4 languages, 40-year resistance vs Portugal, allied with Dutch, formed Kilombo (free slave community), died age 81 undefeated. Cross-dressed male harem, female guard. Led battle at 60.
- Yaa Asantewaa Ashanti 1840-1921 - Queen Mother of Ejisu, led War of Golden Stool 1900 vs British when male chiefs wanted surrender. Said "If you men won't fight, we women will". Exiled to Seychelles.
- Taytu Betul Ethiopia 1851-1918 - Empress, strategist at Adwa 1896, forced Italian border favorable, founded Addis Ababa, controlled modern rifles.
- Nanny of Maroons Jamaica 1686-1733 - Ashanti-born, led Jamaican Maroons vs British, used guerrilla warfare, obeah.

### 5.4 Female Spiritual Power
- Isis (Aset) - Egyptian goddess, throne, magic, mother of Horus. Temple of Philae lasted until 550 CE last pagan temple.
- Mami Wata - water goddess across West/Central Africa, mermaid, wealth, beauty, syncretized in diaspora.
- Mbokomu - Tetela, first woman.
- Priestess roles: God's Wife of Amun (Hatshepsut held), powerful celibate priestess controlling Thebes wealth, later took throne.

### 5.5 Matriarchy Evidence
- Berber: Tuareg are matrilineal, property through mother, men wear veil.
- Akan: Largest matrilineal society in world 12 million.
- Bantu: Many double-descent.
- Research: Cheikh Anta Diop argued Africa matriarchal south vs patriarchal north, based on agriculture (hoe vs plow).
