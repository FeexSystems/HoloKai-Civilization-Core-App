## NODE 7: WEST AFRICAN EMPIRES - GOLD & KNOWLEDGE

### 7.1 Ghana Empire - Wagadou (c.300-1100 CE)
- location: Southeastern Mauritania, Western Mali, not modern Ghana. Capital: Kumbi Saleh (Koumbi Saleh)
- population: Kumbi had two cities: Muslim merchant city with 12 mosques, and royal city 6 miles away with sacred forest.
- king_title: Ghana = title meaning "Warrior King", real name of empire Wagadou. Soninke people.
- economy: Taxed gold and salt, controlled Bambuk goldfields. King held monopoly on gold nuggets. Arab geographer Al-Bakri (1067) described king's court: gold-adorned dogs, 200,000 army (40,000 archers).
- decline: Almoravid invasion 1076, plus climate shift and shift of trade routes.

### 7.2 Mali Empire (c.1235-1600 CE)
- founder: Sundiata Keita (Lion King), c.1235, Mandinka. Epic of Sundiata - oral history still performed by griots.
- constitution: Kouroukan Fouga (1236) - one of world's oldest constitutions, 44 articles, includes human rights, slavery abolition, environmental protection. UNESCO heritage.
- mansa_musa: Ruled 1312-1337, richest person in history (adjusted). Gold production: Mali produced 50% of world gold. Hajj 1324 to Mecca with 60,000 caravan, 12,000 slaves, 80 camels each carrying 300 lbs gold. Gave so much gold in Cairo he caused inflation for 10 years. Put Mali on Catalan Atlas 1375 map with gold nugget.
- timbuktu: City of 25,000 students at University of Sankore. Library: 700,000 manuscripts. Subjects: astronomy, law, surgery, mathematics.
- administration: Empire divided into provinces ruled by Farba (governors). Professional army, cavalry.

### 7.3 Songhai Empire (c.1460-1591 CE)
- capital: Gao, city on Niger River
- sunni_ali: Sunni Ali Ber (1464-1492), master of river warfare, used 400+ pirogue fleet. Conquered Timbuktu and Djenné.
- askia_muhammad: Askia Muhammad Toure (1493-1528), organized empire, standardized currency, weights, created bureaucracy. Made Islam state religion but tolerated traditional.
- university_system: University of Sankore had 25,000 students, 180 schools, library system with private libraries (Ahmed Baba owned 1600 books - lost when Moroccans invaded).
- fall: Battle of Tondibi 1591 vs Moroccan muskets. Moroccan army used English cannons, Songhai had spears. Led to collapse of large empires.

### 7.4 Ife & Benin - Art & Governance
- ife: Yoruba city-state c.1000-1400 CE. Famous for naturalistic bronze and terracotta heads made using lost-wax casting, glass beads. Heads show advanced metallurgy: copper alloy with 12-15% zinc. Evidence of sacred kingship (Ooni).
- benin_empire: Edo people, c.1180-1897 CE. Capital Benin City described by Portuguese 1485 as vast city with walls 16,000km long (largest earthwork in world, 4x Great Wall of China). Walls were 4x longer than Great Wall by volume.
- benin_bronzes: 3000+ brass plaques, portrait heads, made from 13th century. Lost-wax method. Show Oba (king) with Portuguese, leopards. Looted 1897 by British.
- governance: Oba as divine king, but checked by Uzama (kingmakers) and town chiefs. Guild system (Igun Eronmwon brass casters guild still exists today).

## NODE 8: CENTRAL & SOUTHERN KINGDOMS

### 8.1 Kingdom of Kongo (c.1390-1914 CE)
- location: Angola, DRC, Congo, Gabon. Capital Mbanza Kongo (Sao Salvador), population 50,000 in 1600s (as large as Lisbon).
- king_title: Manikongo. Elected from royal clan.
- diplomacy: Early contact with Portugal 1483, King Nzinga a Nkuwu baptized 1491 as Joao I. Had ambassadors in Vatican, Brazil, Lisbon. Sent letters to Pope.
- economy: Raffia cloth currency, copper, ivory. Later destroyed by slave trade.
- text: Letters of Afonso I (Mvemba a Nzinga) c.1526 protesting slave trade - earliest African anti-slavery letters.

### 8.2 Mapungubwe & Great Zimbabwe Continuum
- mapungubwe: c.1075-1220 CE, Limpopo Valley, South Africa. First class-based society in Southern Africa. Famous golden rhino, golden sceptre. Population 5000. Traded gold with Swahili coast, porcelain from China.
- great_zimbabwe: Successor c.1100-1500 CE. Shona. Great Enclosure: 250m circumference, 11m high, 900k blocks, no mortar. Hill Complex = royal palace. Valley = citizens.
- mutapa: Mutapa Empire (c.1430-1760) - successor, controlled gold trade. Called "Monomotapa" by Portuguese.
- symbolism: Zimbabwe Bird (Chapungu) - soapstone sculptures, 8 birds found, now on Zimbabwe flag.

### 8.3 Swahili City-States (c.100-1500 CE)
- cities: Kilwa Kisiwani (Tanzania), Mombasa, Zanzibar, Lamu, Sofala (Mozambique). Stone towns built from coral rag.
- language: Swahili = Kiswahili, Bantu + Arabic + Persian + Hindi loanwords. Written in Ajami (Arabic script) for 1000 years.
- kilwa: Most powerful, controlled gold trade from Great Zimbabwe. Sultan al-Hasan ibn Sulaiman built Husuni Kubwa palace (largest palace south of Sahara at time), had octagonal swimming pool, 100+ rooms.
- economy: Exported gold, ivory, slaves, ambergris, iron. Imported Chinese porcelain (Ming), Indian cotton, Persian glass. Mosque at Kilwa has coral dome with 16th-century inscriptions.
- ibn_battuta: Visited Kilwa 1331, described it as "one of finest and most substantially built towns in world".

## NODE 9: EAST AFRICAN & HORN CONTINUITY

### 9.1 Ethiopian Empire (Solomonic 1270-1974 CE)
- legend: Descent from Menelik I, son of Solomon and Sheba. Keeps Ark of Covenant in Aksum.
- rock_churches: Lalibela 11 churches (see Vol 1) - but also Tigray churches carved 4th century onwards.
- manuscript_culture: 200,000+ Ge'ez manuscripts in monasteries, including Book of Enoch preserved only in Ethiopia.
- resistance: Only African country never colonized (except brief Italian 1935-41). Battle of Adwa 1896 - Ethiopia defeated Italy.

### 9.2 Nubia After Meroe
- christian_nubia: Three kingdoms - Nobatia, Makuria, Alodia c.500-1500 CE. Makuria defeated Arab invasion 642 and 652, forced Baqt treaty - only treaty Arabs signed where they paid tribute (slaves in exchange for grain). Lasted 700 years.
- frescoes: Cathedrals at Faras had 200+ frescoes, Byzantine style with African features.
