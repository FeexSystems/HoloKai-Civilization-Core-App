## NODE 10: UNIVERSITIES & MANUSCRIPT CULTURE

### 10.1 University of Sankore - Timbuktu (c.989 CE - present)
- founding: Mosque founded 989 CE by Al Qadi Aqib ibn Mahmud. University grew around it.
- scale: 25,000 students, 180 schools, 700,000 manuscripts in Timbuktu region. Private libraries: Ahmed Baba library 1600 books.
- curriculum: Quadrivium - Astronomy (ilm al-falak), Mathematics (hisab), Medicine (tibb), Law (fiqh), plus Arabic grammar, rhetoric, geography. Degrees: Ijaza (license to teach).
- method: Students sat in circles (halqa), memorized, debated. Griot system preserved oral history as parallel university.
- manuscripts_content: Mathematics including magic squares, inheritance law calculations, astronomy with planetary charts, medicine with cataract surgery.

### 10.2 Other Universities
- al_azhar: Founded 970 CE in Cairo by Fatimids, originally for Shia theology, now Sunni world's oldest university. Founded by African dynasty.
- fez_al_qarawiyyin: Founded 859 CE by Fatima al-Fihri (Tunisian woman) in Morocco - recognized by Guinness as oldest continuously operating university.
- ethiopian_monasteries: Debre Damo (6th century), Lake Tana monasteries - preserved 200k manuscripts including astronomy, medicine.

### 10.3 Manuscript Preservation
- ahmed_baba: Ahmed Baba al-Massufi (1556-1627), most famous scholar of Timbuktu. Wrote 40+ books. When Moroccans arrested him, he said: "You may have taken my library but my library is in my mind". His books on law and astronomy still studied.
- chinguetti: Libraries in Mauritania (Chinguetti, Ouadane) have 6000+ manuscripts on camel skin, some from 11th century. Stored in desert to prevent decay.

## NODE 11: MEDICINE & SURGERY

### 11.1 Ancient Surgery
- edwin_smith: 48 cases, includes stitching, setting fractures, treating spinal injuries. Mentions meninges, brain pulsations.
- cataract: Egyptian and Malian surgeons performed cataract couching (pushing lens). Timbuktu manuscripts describe eye surgery instruments.
- cesarean: Banyoro of Uganda and Bunyoro performed cesarean sections with antiseptic (banana wine), anesthesia (herbal), and cautery. Observed by Robert Felkin 1879, mother and baby survived. European cesarean mortality then was 80%+.

### 11.2 Pharmacology
- plants: 400+ plants in Egyptian papyri: acacia (gum arabic still used), aloe, garlic (antibiotic), willow (salicylic acid = aspirin precursor).
- ethiopian: Kosso (Hagenia abyssinica) for tapeworm, still used. Over 1000 medicinal plants documented in Ethiopian pharmacopoeia.
- dogon: Knowledge of psychoactive plants for rituals, dosage control.

### 11.3 Public Health
- egypt: Workers at Deir el-Medina had sick leave, workers compensation, organized medical care. Papyri show first strike in history over delayed rations.
- benin: City had street lighting (oil lamps), organized waste disposal, moat system for sewage, 16th century European visitors said it was cleaner than European cities.

## NODE 12: ENGINEERING & NAVIGATION

### 12.1 Architecture Engineering
- pyramid_alignment: Great Pyramid aligned to true north with error 0.06 degrees. Used star alignment (Mizar and Kochab). Base level within 2cm.
- great_zimbabwe_acoustics: Walls built to amplify sound - clap inside Great Enclosure heard at hill complex.
- djenné: Sudano-Sahelian architecture - earth brick, wooden toron poles provide structural support + scaffolding + thermal regulation. Annual replastering keeps building cool.
- aksumite_engineering: How to quarry 500-ton stelae? Used fire-setting (heat rock then quench), dolerite balls, 400+ workers. Transported on rollers lubricated with water.

### 12.2 Metallurgy Details for Tech Tree
- furnace_types: Pit furnace (Nok), Bowl furnace (Meroe), Shaft furnace (Haya). Temperatures: 1100C-1300C. Used bellows made of goat skin, forced draft via tuyeres.
- steel_process: Haya people (Tanzania) made carbon steel by preheating air in furnace - process similar to Bessemer 1800 years later. Produced tools that were harder than European.
- brass_benin: Benin bronzes are actually brass (copper + zinc). Zinc came from trans-Saharan trade. Alloy ratio: 80% copper, 10% zinc, 8% lead - perfect for lost-wax.

### 12.3 Navigation
- polynesian_comparison: Swahili navigators used monsoon winds, star compass, and knowledge of currents to sail to India. Used outrigger canoes (mtepe - sewn boats, no nails, coconut fiber).
- egyptian_navigation: Used merkhet (star clock) and bay (plumb line) for alignment, sun shadow for time.
- sahara_navigation: Tuareg used stars (especially Polaris), wind patterns, dune shapes. Guide called innunaghen (path finders).

### 12.4 Mathematics Applications
- fractal_architecture: Ba-ila village (Zambia) built in fractal pattern - self-similar scaling. Ron Eglash documented African fractals in architecture, hair braiding, settlement planning.
- mancala: Game of strategy, oldest board game (7000 years), based on sowing and capture, teaches math and probability.
- lusona_ideographs: Chokwe people of Angola/Zambia draw continuous line drawings in sand (lusona) - graph theory, Eulerian trails, taught in initiation.

## NODE 13: RESISTANCE & CONTINUITY (FOR MEMORY CORE CONTEXT)

### 13.1 Anti-Colonial Resistance Seed
- amanirenas_vs_rome: First successful resistance vs superpower.
- ethiopia_adwa: 1896 Battle of Adwa - Ethiopia under Menelik II defeated Italy, kept independence. First African victory over European colonizer.
- queen_nzinga: Nzinga of Ndongo and Matamba (Angola, 1583-1663) - fought Portuguese for 40 years, allied with Dutch, brilliant diplomat, led army at age 60.

### 13.2 Knowledge Survival
- how_knowledge_survived: Oral (griots, Ifa corpus of Yoruba - 256 odu with 800k verses memorized), Manuscript (Timbuktu), Architectural (walls that encode math).
- ifa_corpus: Yoruba Ifa divination system - binary system, 256 odu, each with stories, used for governance, medicine. UNESCO heritage. Mathematically similar to computer binary.
