### 7.1 Pyramid Engineering Deep
- great_pyramid: 2.3M blocks, 146.6m, base 230.3m, error 0.06 deg to north, base level within 2.1cm. Built 20 years, 20k workers paid with bread/beer/onions, medical care, village with cemetery shows respect.
- construction: Evidence: water-lubricated sand ramps (painting from Djehutihotep shows water poured before sled reduces friction 50%), lever, copper tools + dolerite balls to cut granite.
- nubian_pyramids: 200+ in Sudan, Meroe, Nuri, El-Kurru, steeper 70 deg, smaller 8m base, chapel facing east, burial inside not below. Built for Kandakes too.
- other_pyramids: Zinder? Not. But: Pyramid of Cestius Rome copied Kush.

### 7.2 Great Zimbabwe Engineering
- walls: Great Enclosure 250m circumference, 11m high, 5m thick at base, 900k blocks, no mortar, built with dry stone, curves inward for stability, drainage weep holes.
- acoustics: Clap inside enclosure heard on hill 80m away - intentional acoustic design for ceremonies.
- conical_tower: 10m high, 5m diameter, solid, purpose debated: grain bin model, phallus, granary symbol.
- birds: 8 soapstone Zimbabwe Birds, 30cm, Chapungu eagle, stylized, now on flag, currency.
- city_plan: Hill = royal/sacred, Valley = commoners, Great Enclosure = initiation/ritual. Population 18,000.

### 7.3 Benin City - Fractal City
- walls: 16,000km total length of earthworks (moats + walls), 4x Great Wall China by volume, Guinness World Record largest earthwork. Outer wall 10m high, 20ft moat. Built over 600 years by 1M workers.
- city: Portuguese 1485: "Great city, streets wide, houses large, market...". Dutch 1691: "No city in Holland as beautiful, with wide streets...". Street lighting with palm oil lamps. No trash - cleaned.
- fractal: Ron Eglash research: Benin city layout fractal - self-similar scaling, palace at center, streets recursive. Same pattern in Ba-ila Zambia village circular fractal, Yoruba compounds.
- bronze: Lost-wax brass, 3000+ plaques, portrait heads, using 80% copper 10% zinc 8% lead. Guild Igun Eronmwon still active since 1200s.

### 7.4 Rock-Hewn & Coral Architecture
- lalibela: 11 churches carved top-down from tuff, 15m deep, drainage, tunnels connecting. Biete Ghiorgis cross-shaped.
- aksum_stelae: 33m 520 tons single granite, false doors/windows 13 stories, quarried 4km, transported on rollers with water lubrication.
- djenné: Great Mosque, Sudano-Sahelian, adobe + shea butter, toron palm wood poles = scaffolding + decoration + thermal expansion + cooling. Annual replastering festival. World's largest mud building.
- kilwa: Husuni Kubwa palace 100+ rooms, octagonal pool, coral rag, domes. Great Mosque dome 16-sided. Gedi Kenya had flushing toilets 11th century, running water.
- egyptian_temple: Karnak hypostyle hall 134 columns 21m high, aligned to winter solstice sunrise through doorway.

### 7.5 Urban Planning
- kumbi_saleh: Two cities 6 miles apart: merchant Muslim city 12 mosques, royal city sacred forest, separated for religious tolerance.
- mbanza_kongo: Capital Kongo, 50k population 1600, stone buildings, palace enclosure, paved streets, as large as Lisbon.
- timbuktu: Sankore University area, 180 schools, libraries, market, built from banco mud.
