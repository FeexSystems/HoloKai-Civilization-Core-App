### 15.1 Kouroukan Fouga - World's Oldest Human Rights Constitution?

#### Background
- date: 1236 CE, after Battle of Kirina, Sundiata Keita founded Mali Empire, gathered 29 clans of Mandinka at clearing called Kouroukan Fouga (Clearing on Rocky Hill).
- griot: Djeli Mamadou Kouyate preserved orally, written down 1998 by Guinean scholars, UNESCO Intangible Heritage 2009.
- articles: 44 articles in 4 sections.

#### Content - 44 Articles
- social_organization: Article 1: Mandinka society divided into 16 clans of free men, 4 clans of griots, 4 clans of artisans, 5 clans of marabouts. Caste system but protected.
- rights: Article 20: Slaves must be freed, no one can be slave. Abolition of slavery 1236, 600 years before US Emancipation 1863. Article 5: Everyone has right to life and preservation.
- justice: Article 3: No one should be tortured, Article 7: Justice should be impartial, Article 10: Respect for elders.
- environment: Article 37: Respect for forest, Article 38: No burning of forest unnecessarily.
- economy: Article 12: House of slaves abolished, Article 14: No stealing.
- famous: "Don't wrong anybody, respect your neighbor".

#### Comparison
- magna_carta: 1215, but only for barons. Kouroukan Fouga for all people including slaves, women, artisans. Earlier human rights concept.

### 15.3 Ubuntu Law & Restorative Justice

#### Ubuntu
- definition: Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu - person is person through others. Law is to restore community, not punish.
- process: Community gathering, offender and victim speak, goal is reconciliation, not prison. Offender must make amends, community heals.
- modern: Used in South Africa Truth and Reconciliation Commission 1996 Desmond Tutu, Rwanda Gacaca courts.

#### Gacaca - Rwanda
- gacaca: Grass courts, community justice after genocide 1994, 12,000 courts, 1.2M cases, faster than ICC. Based on traditional dispute resolution on grass.

#### Ogboni & Council Systems
- ogboni: Yoruba earth society, can check Oba power, can force Oba to commit suicide if tyrant. Judicial power.
- gbar: Mali empire assembly of 29 clans, democratic.
- lukiiko: Buganda parliament, 52 clans represented.

### 15.4 Trade Law & Islamic Law in Africa

#### Dyula Merchants
- mudarabah: Profit sharing partnership, Islamic finance, used by Dyula merchants West Africa 1000 CE onwards.
- hawala: Transfer money without cash via trust network, still used.
- aman: Safe conduct pass for traders, guaranteed by king.

#### Timbuktu Law
- qadi: Judge, Ahmed Baba wrote law books, 40 books on Maliki law.
- inheritance: Complex mathematics for inheritance shares (Ilm al-Faraid), taught in Sankore.
