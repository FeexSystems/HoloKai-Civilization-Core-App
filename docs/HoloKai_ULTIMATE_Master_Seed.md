## SECTION A: ORIGINS - THE FIRST HUMANS & FIRST MATH

### A1. The Cradle - Paleolithic Africa
- location: Great Rift Valley, Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, South Africa
- evidence: Lucy (Australopithecus afarensis, 3.2M BCE, Ethiopia), Jebel Irhoud Homo sapiens 300k BCE Morocco - oldest Homo sapiens fossils, rewriting origin to 300k years.
- ishango_bone: 20,000 BCE, Lake Edward, Congo/DRC border. Baboon fibula with 3 columns: Column G: 11,13,17,19 (primes between 10-20), Column M: 3,6,4,8,10,5,5,7 (doubling/halving), Column D: 11,21,19,9 (10+1,20+1,20-1,10-1). Interpretation: Lunar calendar, prime number knowledge, base 12. Oldest mathematical artifact in world. Now in Brussels Museum.
- blombos_ochre: 70,000 BCE, South Africa, Blombos Cave. Engraved ochre with geometric patterns - earliest abstract art, earliest human symbolic thought.
- adam_calendar: 75,000 BCE, South Africa, stone circle alignment, controversial but claimed oldest calendar.

### A2. Sahara Before Desert - The Green Sahara
- period: African Humid Period c. 10,000 - 3,000 BCE, Sahara was savanna with lakes, rivers, elephants.
- tassili_n_ajjer: Algeria rock art, 15,000+ paintings, shows swimming, cattle herding, chariots. 10,000 BCE - 100 BCE.
- nabta_playa: Egypt/Sudan border, c.7500-3500 BCE, world's oldest astronomical observatory, stone circle aligned to summer solstice, cattle sacrifices. Predates Stonehenge by 1000 years. Evidence of early calendar and organized religion.
- domestication: African cattle domesticated 8000 BCE in Sahara, independently from Fertile Crescent.

## SECTION B: NILE VALLEY - EGYPT & NUBIA

### B1. Ancient Egypt Deep
- unification: Narmer Palette c.3100 BCE, first historical document, shows unification of Upper and Lower Egypt.
- dynasties: Old Kingdom (Pyramid Age 2686-2181 BCE), Middle Kingdom (2055-1650 BCE), New Kingdom (1550-1070 BCE) - imperial age.
- pyramid_engineering: Great Pyramid: 2.3M blocks, avg 2.5 tons, max 80 tons. Precision: aligned north with 0.06 deg error, base level within 2.1cm over 230m. Workers: Village of Deir el-Medina, paid with bread, beer, sick leave - not slaves. Ramp + lever + water lubrication.
- female_pharaohs: Sobekneferu (12th Dyn, 1760 BCE) first confirmed, Hatshepsut (18th Dyn, 22 years), Neferneferuaten (Nefertiti, 1334-1332 BCE), Twosret (19th Dyn), Cleopatra VII Philopator (51-30 BCE, last).
- knowledge: Rhind Papyrus 1650 BCE (84 math problems), Edwin Smith Papyrus 1600 BCE (48 surgical cases - rational medicine), Ebers Papyrus 1550 BCE (700 remedies). Imhotep (c.2670 BCE) - first physician, architect of Step Pyramid, later deified as god of medicine.
- library: House of Life (Per-Ankh) - temple universities with astronomy, medicine, geography.

### B2. Nubia & Kush - The Black Pharaohs
- kerma: Kingdom of Kerma (2500-1500 BCE), capital Kerma, huge mud-brick deffufa (temple 18m high), first large African kingdom south of Egypt.
- kush_conquest: Kushite king Piye (747-716 BCE) conquered Egypt, founded 25th Dynasty, ruled Egypt 100 years. Pharaoh Taharqa (690-664 BCE) built pyramids at Nuri, fought Assyrians.
- meroe: Capital Meroe (590 BCE - 350 CE), 200+ pyramids (steeper 70 deg), iron industry - slag heaps still 10m high. Called "Birmingham of Africa". Had own alphabet Meroitic (300 BCE-400 CE), still partially undeciphered. Only African script south of Sahara in ancient times.
- kandakes: Queen title Kandake. Amanirenas (40-10 BCE) - fought Rome, blind in one eye, buried Augustus bronze head under temple steps. Amanishakheto (10 BCE -1 CE) - gold hoard of 1000+ objects. Amanitore (1-20 CE) - co-ruler with Natakamani, built temples.
- christian_nubia: Three Christian kingdoms 500-1500 CE: Nobatia, Makuria, Alodia. Makuria defeated Arab invasion 642 and 652 CE at Battle of Dongola, forced Baqt treaty - only treaty where Arabs paid tribute (wheat, cloth) to Africans for slaves. Lasted 700 years. Cathedrals at Faras with 200+ Byzantine-style frescoes showing black bishops.

### B3. Land of Punt & Red Sea
- punt: Trade partner from 2500 BCE, source of myrrh, frankincense, ebony, gold, leopard skins. Location likely Eritrea/Ethiopia/Somalia. Hatshepsut expedition Year 9 (c.1470 BCE): 5 ships, 31 live myrrh trees transplanted to Thebes - first botanical garden attempt.
- adulis: Port of Aksum, described in Periplus 50 CE, traded ivory, gold, frankincense for Roman glass, Indian steel.

## SECTION C: NORTH AFRICA & SAHARA

### C1. Carthage & Berber Kingdoms
- carthage: Founded 814 BCE by Phoenician queen Dido/Elissa from Tyre. Capital Tunis. Maritime empire, controlled Sicily, Sardinia, Spain. Population 400k at peak. Innovations: Cothon (circular war harbor), agriculture treatise by Mago (28 books, translated by Rome). Hannibal Barca (247-183 BCE) crossed Alps with elephants, defeated Rome at Cannae 216 BCE.
- garamantes: Saharan kingdom Fezzan Libya 1000 BCE-700 CE. Built 600km foggara underground tunnels to tap fossil water. Capital Garama pop 4000. Used chariots, later cavalry. Controlled trans-Saharan trade before camels. Herodotus called them "great nation".
- numidia: Berber kingdom, Masinissa (238-148 BCE) unified Numidia, cavalry decided Second Punic War. Jugurtha War vs Rome 112-106 BCE.
- mauretania: Kingdom in Morocco/Algeria, King Juba II (50 BCE-24 CE) - scholar king, married Cleopatra Selene II (daughter of Cleopatra VII), wrote books on history, botany. Capital Volubilis with Roman mosaics.

### C2. Tifinagh & Libyco-Berber
- script: Libyco-Berber c.200 BCE, ancestor of Tifinagh. 1000+ inscriptions in Sahara. Written vertically bottom to top originally. Modern Neo-Tifinagh official in Morocco 2003.

## SECTION D: HORN OF AFRICA - AKSUM & ETHIOPIA

### D1. Aksum Empire (c.100-940 CE)
- location: Tigray Ethiopia, Eritrea. Capital Aksum.
- economy: Controlled Red Sea trade, minted own coins (gold, silver, bronze) with Greek and Ge'ez legends - only ancient African empire south of Sahara to mint coins. Coins found in India and China.
- obelisks: Stelae - tallest single stones ever erected. King Ezana Stela 21m standing, Great Stela 33m 520 tons collapsed while being erected. Carved as 13-story buildings with false doors, windows.
- religion: King Ezana converted to Christianity 330 CE, made Christianity state religion before Rome did officially. Ethiopian Church claims Ark of Covenant in Aksum.
- script: Ge'ez abugida, 26 base x 7 vowels = 182 signs, own numerals, still liturgical language. Oldest Ge'ez inscriptions 100 BCE.
- fall: Climate change, Persian and Arab control of Red Sea trade, moved capital inland.

### D2. Ethiopia Solomonic Continuity
- lalibela: 11 rock-hewn churches 12th century, King Lalibela, carved top-down from solid tuff. Biete Ghiorgis cross-shaped 15m deep.
- manuscripts: 200,000+ manuscripts in monasteries, Debre Damo 6th century (rope climb only), Lake Tana. Book of Enoch preserved only in Ethiopia, part of Ethiopian Bible (81 books vs 66 in West).
- adwa: Battle of Adwa 1896 - Menelik II defeated Italy, only African victory over European colonizer during Scramble, preserved independence.

## SECTION E: WEST AFRICAN CLASSICAL CIVILIZATIONS

### E1. Nok Culture (1500 BCE - 500 CE) - Nigeria
- location: Jos Plateau, Nigeria.
- art: Terracotta sculptures, 10cm to life-size, highly stylized eyes, elaborate hairstyles. Made using coiling and carving.
- iron: Earliest iron smelting in West Africa, Taruga site 500 BCE, furnaces 1200-1300C, produced carbon steel. Evidence of independent invention of iron (not from outside).
- mystery: Sudden disappearance around 500 CE - climate?

### E2. Ghana/Wagadou Empire (c.300-1100 CE)
- people: Soninke.
- capital: Kumbi Saleh, two cities - Muslim merchant city with 12 mosques, royal city 6 miles away with sacred forest and gold.
- king_title: Ghana = title meaning Warrior King.
- wealth: Al-Bakri 1067 described king adorned with gold, holding gold nuggets, dogs with gold collars. Army 200,000 (40,000 archers). Taxed gold and salt double. Controlled Bambuk and Bure goldfields - source of 50% world gold at time.
- decline: Almoravid attack 1076, plus shift of trade routes and overgrazing.

### E3. Mali Empire (c.1235-1600 CE) - The Golden Empire
- founder: Sundiata Keita (c.1217-1255), Mandinka prince, defeated Sumanguru at Battle of Kirina 1235. Epic of Sundiata preserved by griots (jeli) - oral epic with music, still performed.
- constitution: Kouroukan Fouga 1236, 44 articles, UNESCO Intangible Heritage. Included: abolition of slavery (Article 20), right to food, environmental protection, justice. World's one of oldest constitutions.
- mansa_musa: Musa Keita I, r.1312-1337, richest human ever (net worth $400B adjusted). Production: Mali produced 50% world gold. Hajj 1324: caravan 60,000 people, 12,000 slaves, 80 camels each 300 lbs gold, gave so much gold in Cairo caused inflation 10 years. On Catalan Atlas 1375 depicted with gold nugget, crown, sceptre - Europe knew Mali as source of gold.
- administration: Empire divided into provinces ruled by Farba, cavalry, professional army. Gbara (assembly) of 29 clans.
- timbuktu: Population 50,000, university Sankore 25,000 students, 180 schools, library 700,000 manuscripts, 150+ schools. Subjects: astronomy, mathematics (magic squares, inheritance law), medicine, geography (Al-Idrisi maps), law.
- griots: Oral historians, keep genealogy, law, history. Role: "blood of society". Balafo (xylophone) invention.

### E4. Songhai Empire (1460-1591 CE) - Largest in African History
- size: 1.4 million sq km, from Atlantic to Hausaland.
- sunni_ali: Sunni Ali Ber 1464-1492, 30-year reign, river admiral, 400 pirogue fleet, conquered Timbuktu 1468 and Djenné 1473 after 7-year siege. Called Sunni Ali the Great.
- askia_muhammad: Askia Muhammad Toure 1493-1528, ex-slave general who overthrew Sunni Ali son. Organized empire: bureaucracy, standardized currency (cowries), weights, measures. Divided into provinces with governors. Created professional army and navy. Made Islam state religion but tolerated traditional.
- university: Sankore continued, Ahmed Baba (1556-1627) wrote 40+ books, library 1600 books personal. When Moroccans invaded 1591, he said "You may take my books but my library is in my mind".
- fall: Battle of Tondibi 1591 vs Moroccan army with English muskets and cannons. Songhai had spears, lost due to gunpowder revolution. Led to Moroccan Pashalik of Timbuktu.

### E5. Ife & Benin - Forest Kingdoms of Art
- ife: Yoruba city-state c.1000-1400 CE. Art: Naturalistic bronze, terracotta, glass beads using lost-wax casting. Heads show realistic faces, scarification. Copper alloy with 12-15% zinc, lead. Sacred kingship Ooni, considered descendant of Oduduwa.
- benin: Edo people, c.1180-1897 CE. Capital Benin City: Portuguese 1485 described as city with walls 16,000km total length (earthworks), moat 20ft deep, streets with ambient light from oil lamps, cleaner than Lisbon. Largest earthwork in world, 4x Great Wall of China by volume (Guinness). Fractal city planning.
- bronzes: 3000+ brass plaques, portrait heads, made from 13th century using lost-wax. Show Oba, Queen Mother Idia (first queen mother), Portuguese with guns, leopards, Europeans. Alloy: 80% copper, 10% zinc, 8% lead. Looted 1897 by British Punitive Expedition, now in British Museum, many being repatriated.
- governance: Oba divine king, but checked by Uzama (7 kingmakers) and guilds. Guilds still exist: Igun Eronmwon (brass casters), wood carvers. Queen Mother Idia important - first queen mother to go to war.

### E6. Hausa City-States & Kanem-Bornu
- hausa: Seven true Hausa states (Hausa Bakwai): Daura, Kano, Katsina, Zaria, Gobir, Rano, Biram. Kano walls 14km, built 11th century. Known for indigo dyeing, leather work, Islamic scholarship.
- kanem_bornu: Kanem Empire c.700-1380 CE, Bornu c.1380-1893 CE, Chad/Nigeria. Longest-lived empire in Africa (1200 years). Mai Dunama Dabbalemi (1221-1259) controlled Fezzan, had diplomatic relations with Tunisia. Army with 30,000 cavalry. Capital Njimi, later Ngazargamu. Scholars like Al-Kanemi.

## SECTION F: CENTRAL & GREAT LAKES

### F1. Kongo Kingdom (c.1390-1914 CE)
- location: Angola, DRC, Republic of Congo, Gabon.
- capital: Mbanza Kongo (Sao Salvador), population 50,000 in 1600 (as large as Lisbon), had paved streets, stone buildings, royal palace with enclosure.
- manikongo: Title of king, elected from royal clan. Could be destooled.
- diplomacy: Contact with Portugal 1483, King Nzinga a Nkuwu baptized 1491 as Joao I. Sent ambassador to Vatican 1491, had embassies in Lisbon, Brazil, Rome. King Afonso I (Mvemba a Nzinga) r.1509-1543, wrote letters in Portuguese to Portuguese king protesting slave trade 1526 - earliest African anti-slavery letters: "We need no more slaves from our country".
- economy: Raffia cloth currency (mpu), copper, ivory, nzimbu shells. Textiles so fine Europeans thought they were silk.
- christianity: Syncretic Christianity, Saint Anthony movement by Dona Beatriz Kimpa Vita 1706 - first African Christian prophetess, burned as heretic.

### F2. Luba & Lunda Empires (1585-1885 CE)
- luba: Central DRC, c.1585-1889, founder Kalala Ilunga. Sacred kingship, memory boards (lukasa) - wooden boards with beads encoding history, proverbs, geography - used as mnemonic device for court historians. Like computer memory.
- lunda: c.1600-1887, founder Mwaant Yaav, empire stretched Angola to Zambia, 300,000 sq km, controlled copper and salt trade. Used Cokwe art.

### F3. Buganda & Great Lakes
- buganda: Kingdom Uganda c.14th century-present, Kabaka (king), advanced navy on Lake Victoria, 52 clans, parliament (Lukiiko), advanced bark cloth manufacturing (UNESCO).
- rwanda: Banyarwanda kingdom, Mwami king, intore warriors, advanced cattle culture, oral history via ibitekerezo.

## SECTION G: SOUTHERN AFRICA

### G1. Mapungubwe (1075-1220 CE)
- location: Limpopo Valley, South Africa/Zimbabwe/Botswana border.
- significance: First class-based society in Southern Africa, transition from egalitarian to king and commoners.
- artifacts: Golden rhino (800g gold), golden sceptre, golden bowl - evidence of king. Population 5000. Traded gold, ivory, glass beads from India.
- decline: Climate shift, moved to Great Zimbabwe.

### G2. Great Zimbabwe (1100-1500 CE) - The Stone City
- builders: Shona ancestors, Karanga.
- structures: Hill Complex (royal, with 8 Zimbabwe Birds - soapstone birds 30cm, symbol of Chapungu eagle, now national emblem), Valley Complex (commoners), Great Enclosure: 250m circumference, 11m high, 5m thick, 900,000 granite blocks, no mortar, built with dry stone technique. Walls curve inward for stability. Conical tower 10m high solid, purpose unknown (grain bin? phallus?).
- engineering: Acoustic properties - clap inside enclosure heard on hill. Drainage system, winter/summer palaces for climate control.
- economy: Gold mines (700+ mines), ivory, traded via Swahili city Kilwa to China - Ming porcelain, Persian pottery found.
- population: 18,000 at peak, largest city south of Sahara.
- mutapa: Successor Mutapa Empire 1430-1760, called Monomotapa by Portuguese, controlled gold, had army of 30,000 including female archers.

### G3. Khoisan & Early Southern
- san: San people rock art 20,000+ sites, Drakensberg paintings show shamanic trance, earliest astronomy knowledge.
- zulu_precursor: Nguni migrations, cattle culture, age-grade system.

## SECTION H: SWAHILI COAST - AFRICA & INDIAN OCEAN

### H1. Swahili City-States (c.100-1500 CE)
- cities: Mogadishu (Somalia), Lamu, Mombasa, Malindi, Gedi, Zanzibar, Kilwa Kisiwani, Sofala.
- language: Kiswahili - Bantu grammar + Arabic, Persian, Hindi loanwords. First written in Ajami Arabic script 1000 years, later Latin. Means "coast".
- architecture: Coral rag stone, lime mortar, mangrove poles, carved doors (Zanzibar doors with spikes to prevent elephants).
- kilwa_deep: Most powerful c.1200-1400. Sultan al-Hasan ibn Sulaiman built Husuni Kubwa palace (largest palace south of Sahara at time) - 100+ rooms, octagonal pool, domes. Great Mosque at Kilwa - coral dome, 16-sided dome. Controlled gold trade from Great Zimbabwe. Ibn Battuta visited 1331: "Kilwa is one of finest and most substantially built towns in world, all of wood? No, coral".
- economy: Exported gold, ivory, slaves, ambergris, leopard skins, iron. Imported Chinese porcelain (Ming, Qing), Indian cotton (calico), Persian glass, beads.
- navigation: Used mtepe - sewn boats with coconut fiber, no nails, flexible for reefs. Used monsoon winds, star compass, understood currents.
- gedi_ruins: Kenya, abandoned city 1400s, had running water, flushing toilets, 11th century.
