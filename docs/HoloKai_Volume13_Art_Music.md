### 13.1 Visual Arts - Sculpture Traditions

#### Nok Terracotta (1500 BCE-500 CE)
- style: Highly stylized, triangular eyes, elaborate hairstyles, coiffure, jewelry. Made with coiling, 10cm to life-size. Purpose: ancestor, ritual.
- technique: Fired at 600C, hollow, coil-built.

#### Ife Naturalism (1000-1400 CE)
- style: Hyper-naturalistic, realistic faces, scarification, serene, life-size bronze heads with glass beads. Copper alloy 12-15% zinc. Lost-wax casting perfect.
- meaning: Ooni sacred king, portrait heads for ancestor veneration, ideal beauty.

#### Benin Bronzes (1200-1897 CE)
- style: 3000+ plaques, portrait heads, idealized Oba with coral beads, leopards, Portuguese. Brass 80% copper 10% zinc 8% lead from manillas. Lost-wax.
- guild: Igun Eronmwon brass casters guild still active since 1200s, hereditary, secret techniques, only for Oba.

#### Other Arts
- djenné_terracotta: 13th century Mali, figures with hands on knees, serpents.
- akan_goldweights: Brass weights with proverbs, each weight = proverb, 1000+ designs, physical proverb library.
- kuba_cloth: Congo, raffia cloth, geometric applique, velvet, king's cloth.
- san_rock_art: 20k sites, trance, healing, animals.

### 13.2 Music - Instruments That Changed World

#### Kora
- origin: Mandinka, 18th century, 21-string harp-lute, gourd + cow skin + rosewood, strings fishing line originally gut. Played by griot, 2 hands like piano. Range 3 octaves. Storytelling.

#### Balafon
- origin: 12th century Mali, wooden xylophone, 27 keys, gourds with spider web for buzz, played with 2 mallets. Origin of xylophone and marimba. Balla Fasséké first griot played.

#### Ngoni / Xalam / Banjo
- ngoni: 4-string lute, hunter's instrument, ancestor of banjo via slave trade. Donso ngoni 6 strings.

#### Talking Drums
- dundun: Yoruba hourglass drum, squeeze to change pitch, mimics tone language, sends messages 10km.
- atumpan: Akan talking drums, male/female pair, used for history.
- sabar: Wolof drums, 20 drums ensemble.

#### Other
- mbira: Shona thumb piano, 22-28 keys, metal tines on wood, used to contact ancestors, polyrhythmic.
- algaita: Hausa oboe, loud, royal.
- flutes: Fulani flute, 3-hole.

### 13.3 Rhythm & Fractals
- polyrhythm: African music uses 3:2, 4:3 cross-rhythm, multiple rhythms simultaneously, basis of jazz, blues, rock.
- fractal_music: Same pattern as architecture, Ron Eglash documented fractal rhythms.
- call_response: Leader calls, chorus responds - democratic, communal.
