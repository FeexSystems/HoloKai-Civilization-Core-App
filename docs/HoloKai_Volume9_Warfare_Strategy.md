### 9.1 Ancient & Classical Warfare

#### Egyptian & Nubian Military
- new_kingdom_army: First professional army c.1550 BCE, chariots, composite bow (200m range), khopesh sickle sword.
- nubian_archers: Called "Ta-Sety - Land of Bow" by Egyptians, Nubian archers most feared, used poison arrows, longbow.
- kushite_tactics: Kush used war elephants earlier than Carthage, Meroitic cavalry, iron weapons vs bronze.
- amanirenas_war: Meroitic-Roman War 27-22 BCE, Amanirenas led 30,000, destroyed statues of Augustus at Elephantine, buried head under temple steps, forced treaty where Rome paid tribute to Kush via trade.

#### Carthage - Hannibal Strategy
- hannibal_barca: 247-183 BCE, greatest strategist antiquity. Army: Numidian light cavalry, Iberian infantry, Libyan spearmen, elephants (African forest elephants smaller than savanna).
- crossing_alps: 218 BCE, crossed Alps in 15 days with 38 elephants, 50k infantry, 9k cavalry, lost half to cold.
- cannae: Battle of Cannae 216 BCE - double envelopment, 50k Romans killed in one day, studied at West Point still. Used crescent formation.
- numidian_cavalry: Light cavalry without saddle, turned battle at Zama, later became Roman cavalry core.

### 9.2 West African Warfare

#### Mali & Songhai
- mali_army: Mansa Musa army 100,000 including 10,000 cavalry (sofas), infantry with poison arrows. Used chain mail imported via trans-Saharan, but also local quilted armor (cotton padded, could stop arrows).
- songhai_river_navy: Sunni Ali Ber created 400+ pirogue fleet on Niger River, each 30m long, 50 men, used for rapid deployment - first river navy in Africa, controlled 1000km river.
- battle_tondibi: 1591 Battle of Tondibi - Songhai vs Morocco. Moroccans had 4000 muskets, English cannons. Songhai had 30,000 infantry, 10k cavalry. Morocco won due to gunpowder, stampeding cattle used by Songhai failed because gunfire scared them. Lesson: tech shift ends empire.

#### Benin & Yoruba
- benin_army: Oba army with Portuguese matchlocks from 1485, used iron wall of Benin as defense, 16,000km earthworks. Also used poison arrows, leopard.
- oyo_cavalry: Oyo Empire (1300-1896) cavalry empire, used horses from north, controlled savanna, could not enter forest due to tsetse fly killing horses - ecological limit of empire.
- dahomey_amazons: Agojie, female warriors of Dahomey (Benin), 6000 women, recruited from age 13, celibate, called "our mothers", fought French 1890-1894, used muskets, machetes, inspired Black Panther Dora Milaje. Last amazon died 1979 (Nawi).

### 9.3 Central & Southern Warfare

#### Kongo & Angola
- kongo_army: 70,000 army including 30,000 archers, used shield + sword, copper armor. Battle of Mbwila 1665 vs Portuguese - Kongo lost due to muskets.
- nzinga_strategy: Queen Nzinga Angola 1583-1663 - 40 years guerrilla warfare vs Portugal, allied with Dutch 1641 to retake Luanda, formed Kilombo (Quilombo) free slave communities, used scorched earth, spies, negotiated as equal with Portuguese governor while sitting on servant's back (refused to sit on floor). Died undefeated age 81.

#### Zulu Military Revolution - Shaka
- shaka_zulu: Shaka kaSenzangakhona 1787-1828, reformed army 1816-1828, created disciplined regiments (impi), age-grade system.
- innovations: Iklwa short stabbing spear (instead of throwing assegai, for close combat), larger cowhide shield, barefoot for mobility (thorns removed feet hardened), "bullhorn" formation - chest (main force), horns (encircle), loins (reserve). Copied by British.
- tactics: No sandals = faster, forced march 80km/day, used psychological warfare, total war. Army 40,000 at peak, controlled 200,000 sq km.
- zulu_victory: Battle of Isandlwana 1879 - Zulu 20,000 defeated British 1800 with Martini-Henry rifles using bullhorn - one of worst British defeats.
- other: Used intelligence network, spies, forced enemy to fight on his terms.

### 9.4 Ethiopian Warfare - Adwa
- adwa_1896: Ethiopia vs Italy, Menelik II + Empress Taytu Betul. Ethiopian army 100,000 (80k infantry, 20k cavalry), many with modern rifles (bought with coffee gold), but also spears. Italian army 14,500 with artillery.
- taytu_strategy: Empress Taytu insisted on attacking at Adwa not waiting, controlled 6000 cavalry reserve, cut water supply to Italians. Ethiopian women supplied water, ammunition.
- result: Italy defeated, 7000 killed, 1200 prisoners, Ethiopia kept independence, only African nation never colonized (except brief 1935-41). Treaty of Addis Ababa - Italy recognized Ethiopia.

### 9.5 Defensive Systems & Fortifications
- walls: Benin walls 16,000km, Great Zimbabwe walls 11m high no mortar, Kano walls 14km 11th century, Kumbi Saleh walls.
- hill_forts: Zimbabwe hill complex, Mapungubwe hill, Ethiopian amba (flat-topped mountain forts) like Debre Damo only rope access.
- forest_defense: Yoruba used forest as defense vs cavalry, Oyo couldn't enter forest due to tsetse fly.
- desert: Garamantes foggara tunnels hidden, Tuareg desert knowledge as weapon.

### 9.6 Strategy Concepts for HoloKai
- african_strategy_principles: 
1. Mobility over mass (Numidian cavalry, Zulu barefoot)
2. Environment as weapon (Sahara, forest, tsetse, rivers)
3. Intelligence & spies (Nzinga network, Shaka scouts)
4. Psychological (Shaka total war, Benin leopard symbolism)
5. Asymmetric (Amanirenas vs Rome, guerrilla)
- sun_tzu_comparison: Many principles parallel Sun Tzu but earlier: Sunni Ali river control = control center line, Shaka bullhorn = double envelopment like Hannibal Cannae.
