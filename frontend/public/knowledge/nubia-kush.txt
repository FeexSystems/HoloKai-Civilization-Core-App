NUBIA AND THE KINGDOM OF KUSH (Sudan)

Nubia, located in present-day Sudan, was home to one of Africa's oldest civilizations. The Kingdom of Kush ruled from Kerma (2500 BCE), Napata (750 BCE), and Meroe (300 BCE).

KEY ACHIEVEMENTS:
- More pyramids than Egypt — over 200 at Meroe alone
- Ruled Egypt as the 25th Dynasty (Black Pharaohs: Piye, Shabaka, Taharqa)
- Advanced iron smelting — Meroe was the "Birmingham of Africa"
- Meroitic script — an indigenous writing system (still not fully deciphered)
- Controlled trade routes and major gold mining operations

WOMEN IN POWER:
- Kandakes (warrior queens) ruled Kush
- Amanirenas defeated Roman forces (c. 25 BCE)
- One of the few ancient civilizations where women regularly held supreme power

SIGNIFICANCE:
- Proof of an independent African civilization rivaling Egypt
- Challenge to the "Egypt as non-African" narrative
- Rediscovery of Nubian civilization continues to reshape African historiography
