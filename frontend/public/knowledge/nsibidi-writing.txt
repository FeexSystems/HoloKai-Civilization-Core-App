NSIBIDI WRITING (Efik/Ibibio, Nigeria)

Nsibidi is an indigenous writing system of the Efik, Ibibio, and Ejagham peoples of southeastern Nigeria. One of the few original writing systems invented in sub-Saharan Africa.

FEATURES:
- Logographic and ideographic script
- Over 1,000 known symbols
- Used for recording laws, stories, and esoteric knowledge
- Transmitted through secret societies (Ekpe, Ngbe)
- Dates back at least 500 years

SIGNIFICANCE:
- Represents indigenous African literacy independent of outside influence
- Influenced the development of Afro-Caribbean writing systems like Anaforuana
- Some symbols bear striking resemblance to Adinkra and other West African symbolic systems
- Modern interest in Nsibidi for African futuristic art and design
