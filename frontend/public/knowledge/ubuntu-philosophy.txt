UBUNTU PHILOSOPHY (Southern Africa)

Ubuntu is a Nguni Bantu philosophy meaning "I am because we are." It represents a worldview centered on interconnectedness, community, and shared humanity.

CORE PRINCIPLES:
- Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu — a person is a person through other persons
- The belief that individual well-being is inseparable from community well-being
- Emphasis on compassion, reciprocity, dignity, and mutual respect
- Conflict resolution through dialogue and restoration

KEY EXPONENTS:
- Desmond Tutu: Ubuntu as the foundation of South Africa's Truth and Reconciliation Commission
- John Mbiti: "I am because we are; we are therefore I am"
- Nelson Mandela: Ubuntu as a principle of leadership and governance

GLOBAL RELEVANCE:
- Recognized as a precursor to communitarian philosophy
- Influences modern leadership theory and organizational culture
- Resonates with contemporary movements for collective wellbeing
- A framework for ethical AI development centered on human dignity
