MALI EMPIRE (c. 1235-1600 CE)

The Mali Empire was one of the largest and wealthiest empires in African history, centered in West Africa. Founded by Sundiata Keita after the Battle of Kirina (1235 CE), it became a symbol of African power, scholarship, and prosperity.

KEY ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Controlled the trans-Saharan trade routes in gold, salt, and other goods
- Mansa Musa's pilgrimage to Mecca in 1324-25 demonstrated unmatched wealth
- Timbuktu became a world center of learning with Sankore University
- The Manden Charter (Kurukan Fuga) — one of the world's earliest human rights declarations (c. 1236)
- Advanced agriculture, administration, and a standing army

SCHOLARSHIP IN TIMBUKTU:
- Over 700,000 manuscripts covering astronomy, math, medicine, law, theology
- Scholars from across Africa and the Middle East traveled to study
- Djinguereber, Sankore, and Sidi Yahya mosques were major learning centers

KEY FIGURES:
- Sundiata Keita (c. 1217-1255) — founder
- Mansa Musa (c. 1280-1337) — wealthiest person in history
- Ibn Battuta — visited and documented the empire
