SONGHAI EMPIRE

The Songhai Empire was one of the largest states in African history, dominating the western Sahel and Niger River bend in the 15th–16th centuries. It succeeded Mali as the preeminent West African power, with capitals linked to Gao, Timbuktu, and Djenné.

KEY ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Imperial administration under the Askiya dynasty with provincial governors and a professional military
- Control of trans-Saharan trade in gold, salt, slaves, and scholarly books
- Flourishing of Islamic scholarship at Sankore and other Timbuktu madrasas
- Riverine economy along the Niger supporting agriculture, fishing, and long-distance commerce
- Legal and bureaucratic reforms associated with Askiya Muhammad I
- Urban craft and market systems in Gao, Timbuktu, and Djenné

CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE:
- Songhai consolidated and expanded intellectual networks already famous under Mali
- Arabic literacy and Islamic jurisprudence coexisted with local Songhay language and traditions
- Timbuktu libraries and manuscript culture preserved astronomy, law, medicine, and theology
- The empire demonstrated sophisticated African statecraft independent of European models

KEY FIGURES:
- Sunni Ali (Sonni Ali) — military founder who seized Timbuktu and expanded Songhai power
- Askiya Muhammad I (Askiya the Great) — reorganized administration, promoted Islam and scholarship, performed the Hajj
- Scholars of Timbuktu — jurists, astronomers, and teachers whose manuscripts still survive

DECLINE:
- Moroccan invasion (1591) with firearms at the Battle of Tondibi shattered central authority
- Fragmentation into smaller successor states; scholarly life in Timbuktu continued under new political conditions
- European Atlantic trade gradually reoriented West African commercial gravity toward the coast

LEGACY:
- Songhai is central to Sahelian political memory and West African educational history
- Manuscripts from the region are key sources for African intellectual history
- The empire's scale challenges outdated narratives that denied complex African state formation
