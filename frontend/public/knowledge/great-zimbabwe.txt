GREAT ZIMBABWE (c. 1100-1450 CE)

Great Zimbabwe was a medieval city and capital of the Kingdom of Zimbabwe. The largest stone structure in pre-colonial sub-Saharan Africa and a major Indian Ocean trade power.

KEY ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Massive stone architecture without mortar — Great Enclosure, Hill Complex, Valley Ruins
- Walls up to 11m high spanning 250m
- Controlled gold, ivory, copper trade to Swahili Coast, China, Persia
- Population of 10,000-20,000 at peak

SIGNIFICANCE:
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- "Zimbabwe" means "houses of stone" in Shona
- Challenged colonial narratives about African capabilities
